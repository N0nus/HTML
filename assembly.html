Assembling Your PC

Overview of the Assembly Process

Although assembly seems challenging at first, it is a fairly straightforward task once you get the hang of it. It is generally best for beginners to use a video so that they can follow along with real-time pictures. Hereâ€™s a brief overview of the steps involved:

1. Preparation: Gather all the components and tools you'll need for the build. Ensure you have a clean, static-free workspace.

2. Installing the CPU: Place the CPU into the motherboard socket, aligning it properly with the notches. Secure it with the locking mechanism.

3. Mounting the CPU Cooler: Attach the CPU cooler to the processor after applying thermal paste. Ensure it is securely fastened to prevent overheating.

4. Installing RAM: Insert the RAM sticks into the motherboard slots, ensuring they click into place.

5. Mounting the Motherboard: Secure the motherboard into the case using the provided standoffs and screws.

6. Installing the Power Supply: Place the PSU into the case and connect it to the motherboard and other components.

7. Installing Storage Drives: Mount your hard drives or SSDs into the case and connect them to the motherboard and power supply.

8. Installing the GPU: Insert the graphics card into the appropriate PCIe slot on the motherboard and secure it with screws.

9. Connecting Cables: Connect all necessary cables, including power, data, and front panel connectors.

10. Final Checks: Ensure all components are securely installed and connected. Double-check all cable connections.

11. Power On: Boot up the system and enter the BIOS/UEFI to configure initial settings. Install your operating system and drivers.

This is the general process that nearly all PCs will follow. For more detailed guidance, refer to the videos below.
